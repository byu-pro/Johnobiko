Neue Haas Grotesk: A Typographic Exploration Project Overview

For this project, I was tasked with designing a poster to promote and sell the Neue Haas Grotesk font to professional designers. The objective was to showcase the versatility and potential of this typeface while providing all the necessary information for designers to make an informed purchasing decision.



I was contacted by Fonts.com to promote a font by creating a printed poster to send out to professional designers. The poster was to show the versatility and possibility of the assigned font. It would also contain all the information needed for the designer to make a purchasing decision. The poster's function was to catch someone's eye, inform, and then encourage them to purchase the font.

From the initial concept stage, I focused on creating a strong focal point that would immediately draw the viewer's attention. I achieved this by utilizing a large, bold display of the Neue Haas Grotesk font name as the central element of the composition. This not only established the font as the subject matter but also showcased its versatility in larger display sizes. To maintain visual interest and balance, I carefully explored the interplay of positive and negative space. By allowing ample negative space around the large font display, I created a sense of breathing room and minimalism, letting the typeface take center stage. At the same time, I utilized negative space within the letterforms themselves to add texture and depth to the design.
